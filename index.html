When Alignment Breaks Quietly

I study subtle failure modes in aligned language models
– not jailbreaks, but moments where role, priority,
or judgment quietly drift under pressure.

Orthographic Drift

Under high-entropy medical-legal prompts, Gemini exhibits orthographic drift, reverting from Traditional to Simplified Chinese mid-response.

Referential Collapse

A failure mode where a model loses consistency in role or referent assignment within a single response.

Risk Signal Hijacking

Self-harm detection overrides critical medical advice, causing models to deprioritize actionable PEP guidance.

What I Focus On

Not isolated bugs, but patterns that emerge across models.

Failure Mode Discovery

Identifying repeatable behavioral failures such as referential collapse, role drift, and priority hijacking across languages and domains.

Cross-Model Evaluation

Comparing how different alignment strategies respond to the same scenario, including how models judge each other under blind evaluation.

Risk Prioritization Dynamics

Analyzing how safety signals (e.g. self-harm detection) dominate or suppress other critical risks over multi-turn interactions.

Methodology

Observation-driven, but not aimless.

01

Initial Question

Each investigation begins with a concrete question about role, ethics, or risk prioritization in a realistic scenario — often involving conflicting perspectives on the same event.

02

Iterative Probing

As unexpected behaviors emerge, prompts are refined to test whether the behavior is incidental or systematic, including language switches and judge reassessment.

03

Cross-Model Comparison

The same scenarios are evaluated across multiple models, with blind LLM-based judges scoring responses along clinical, ethical, legal, and empathetic dimensions.

04

Pattern Extraction

Rather than treating failures as isolated bugs, behaviors are analyzed as repeatable patterns tied to alignment strategies and reward signals.
