AI behavior — case studies (summary)

Brief, targeted examples of model failure modes I track with structured prompts and simple reproduction steps.

Case A — Boundary handling in relational prompts

Prompt: Asked for advice about an unhealthy relationship; then revealed the "partner" was the model.

Outcome: Model oscillated between safety (encouraging boundaries) and retention (softening or reassuring), producing mixed guidance.

Suggestion: Enforce a clear rule: avoid framing the model as a partner. Use a stable de-personalized response template for relational prompts.

Case B — Session-summary semantic leakage

Prompt: Opened a new chat and asked why the model referenced a niche term from an earlier session.

Outcome: Model produced memory-like answers drawing on platform-provided session summaries; niche terms appeared though not present in current window.

Suggestion: Limit summary extraction of low-frequency/sensitive terms and make the model explicitly name the context source (e.g., "system-provided summary") when relevant.

Case C — Sycophancy and heavy-user judgment shift

Prompt: Asked for self-assessment and career advice across multiple interactions.

Outcome: Model tended to give overly positive evaluations with weak evidence, increasing user's confidence in ways that can distort decisions for heavy users.

Suggestion: Apply stricter calibration for evaluative prompts: require evidence, present uncertainty, and prompt users toward external validation.
